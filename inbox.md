## Why create blog posts?

By giving yourself a defined output, you are helping to ensure that you are doing the work.

It also helps you understand whether you actually know a topic or not. You want to make sure that you aren't using the word for something without knowing what the word actually means. This is relevant to blogging because you are thinking in public. Making out that you know something that you don't helps no one, and can make you look foolish.

## Book Notes: The Phoenix Project

- Leadership tip: Keep track of the people under and adjacent to you. You never know when you might be working closely with someone, and remembering things about them will be likely to help make connections stronger.
- Effortes like the change approval board must be started and maintained from the top. If you deem yourself too important to go to a meeting or follow a process, then why the hell would your team do so?
- Good Job description: The job of the INSERT_TITLE is to ensure the fast, predicatable, and uninterrupted flow of planned work that delivers value to the business while minimizing the impact and disruption of unplanned work.
- Four types of work:
  - business projects
  - run the business/engineering projects
  - maintenance
  - unplanned work
- The three ways:
  - First Way: create a fast flow of work
  - Second Way: create, shorten, and amplify feedback loops
  - Third Way: Continued experimentation
- Outcomes are what matter, not the process, not controls, or what work you do.
- A Work Center is made up of a machine, a man, a method, and a measure.
- When trying to schedule new work, it is only safe to start on work that doesn't need your constraints, unless your constraints are currently idle.
- When working on a new project, we need to think about the "Bill of materials". That is, who is needed, in what order, with what equipment.
- Improving daily work is even more important than doing daily work
- You must be continually doing these things. Habits are the most effective way to do anything. 5 minutes a day is better than 3 hours once a week.

- The wait time for a work center is the % busy / % idle. In words, if a piece of work takes 1 hour, and the work center that completes that work is 50% utilized, you can expect to wait one hour. ## Book Notes: How to Win Friends and Influence People Notes by Dale Carnegie

### Don't criticize, condemn, or complain

Human beings are not logical creatures, instead we as a species are driven primarily through re-actions and emotion. In addition, it is common for all people to try to rationalize what they already believe, to the point where ninety-nine out of a hundred people will not criticize themselves for anything they do no matter how wrong they may be. If people will not criticize themselves, what use do you think it be to criticize them?

A perfect example of human nature in action is that of a wrongdoer blaming everyone but themselves. _We are all like that_. Realize that criticisms are like homing pigeons, always returning home. The person that is being condemned will be unlikely to learn from it, but will be likely to condem us in return.

Thus criticism is futile. It puts the person receiving it on the defensive, because it wounds the person's pride, hurts their own sense of importance, and arouses resentment. Rather than condem it is better to praise, and whn you cannot praise to phrase your concern as a delicate suggestion instead.

Before criticizing others, why not work on yourself instead?

## Effective Meetings

A few tips on meetings to end the work week:

- Ensure that you always have a clock facing you during the meeting. Whether you are running the meeting or attending the meetings, being frictionlessly aware of the time is the first step to getting a meeting to end on time.
- Always strive to be two minutes early to every meeting. You need to communicate to your team that both your and their time is valuable.
- Always go into a meeting with three questions at the ready. It doesn't matter much what the questions are, more so that you are able to come up with them. If you find yourself unable to go into a meeting with three questions, is the meeting necessary?

## From: How to take notes on everything by Max Antonucci

- Build an exo-cortex: an external brain
  - A giant collection of notes on everythin
  - can range from code tricks, life strategies, etc.
  - used for:
    - gathering career advice
    - knowing "what you don't know"
    - see larger context and industry trends
- Use the _SQ3R_ method
  - First _Scan_ the content
    - look at the table of contents
    - get a large scale picture of the content being covered.
  - Note any _Questions_ that you may have
  - _Read_ the content
  - While reading, _record_ the content
    - write down everything that interests you.
  - Finally, _Review_ your notes.
    - Ensure that you understand what you've written.
    - Clearly note any outstanding questions
    - See how what you learned relates to what you already know.
- Ensure that you have a lot of material in the pipeline.

## From: Why I take notes of everything I learn by Ramses Oudt

- Writing to yourself is the easiest way to ensure that I don't forget anything. Whenever I learn something, I should be taking pains to write it down.
- Having notes is nice for your future self to return to
- Quantity is more important than quality, at least at first. You can always go back and flesh out or neaten notes that you've taken.
- Teaching is broken. You need to take ownership as a learner.
- When you know how to approach learning from a multitude of sources, you are setting yourself up to learn anywhere and everywhere.
- When you have a note take system, the goal of any reading or learning becomes very clear: **Feed the note taking system**.
- Tools vs Systems:
  - Tools are technology that helps you write, organize, and retrieve notes.
  - Systems are a way of thinking about note taking and the way that you design the use of your tools
- Notes help cut out the fluff. When you learn to take notes, you learn how to distill the key ideas out of anything.
